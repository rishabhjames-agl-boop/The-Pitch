The Fine Print

Rules of engagement

Read carefully. No refunds. No appeals. No renegotiation.

I

Thirty minutes

You have thirty minutes to invent your show, build your pitch, and create your prop. The clock does not stop. The clock is not your friend.

II

The One prop

Every team must produce one artifact to support their pitch. A poster, a fake magazine cover, a logo, a mood board, a tabloid front page, a contestant's audition tape on a phone. One prop. No more. No less.

III

Ten Minutes on the floor

You have a maximum of ten minutes to present. The host will cut you off. We are not negotiating.

IV

The Three Cards Are gospel

The three handbook cards you drew must be woven into the pitch. Not mentioned. Woven. We need to see how they shape the show, not hear that they exist.

V

The Five essentials

Every pitch must deliver a title, a tagline, the format, the main character, and the villain. If we leave the room not knowing any of these, the pitch failed.

VI

Commit or die

Whatever you pitch, you sell it like it has already been commissioned for three seasons. Half-pitches do not get green-lit. Confidence is the format.
